Equipment finance FAQs

Common questions about equipment finance through ElevateLend.

With a chattel mortgage, you own the equipment from day one and the lender holds a “mortgage” over it until the loan is repaid. With a hire purchase, the financier owns the equipment and you take ownership at the end of the term. Both have similar rates, but chattel mortgages allow you to claim the GST upfront. Your funding specialist will help you choose the right structure.

Yes. Many lenders on our panel finance both new and used equipment. Rates on used equipment may be slightly higher, and maximum terms may be shorter depending on the age and condition of the asset. Private sales can also be financed in many cases.

Many of our lenders offer 100% finance with no deposit required. A deposit can reduce your repayments and may improve your interest rate, but it is not mandatory in most cases. Your funding specialist will find the best option for your budget.

A balloon (or residual) is a lump sum payment at the end of the loan term. It reduces your monthly repayments during the term but means a larger final payment. At the end, you can pay it off, refinance it, or in some cases, return the equipment. This is common with vehicle finance and operating leases.
